Chapter 04

Four subjects. Four pedagogies.

The same Socratic engine, four different ways of asking. Scroll → to walk through them.

Physics

Which principle applies first?

A ball thrown at 30° with v₀ = 20 m/s — find the range.
Before any numbers — which conservation or kinematic principle fits a projectile? And once you pick it, what are the two component equations you need to write down?
Mathematics

What's the next step you'd write?

Solve ∫ x·sin(x) dx
Two factors multiplied — which technique pairs that pattern? After you pick u and dv, write the first line of integration by parts and let me see it.
English Literature

Where does the poem say so?

What is Keats showing in the first stanza of Hyperion?
Look at "quiet as a stone" and the "voiceless" stream. What do those two images do to Saturn's presence in the scene — and which line tells you he hasn't moved in a long time?
Persuasive Writing

What is your claim — really?

Social media is harmful for teenagers.
Is the harm the screen time itself, or what happens during it? Pick one — then tell me the strongest counter-argument someone in your audience would raise.

How it works

Three steps. No setup.

Vaaani turns whatever you upload into a navigable map of ideas — then teaches across that map.

Subject-aware pedagogy

Tutors that know their subject.

The same Socratic engine, four different pedagogies. Vaaani adapts how it questions you to the material it's working with.

Physics

Asks which principle applies, what's given vs. unknown, and what units the answer should be in. Will not hand you a numeric answer until you've reasoned about the approach.

Mathematics

Asks which technique fits, what the next algebraic step would be, and whether your result passes a sanity check — units, limits, boundary cases. Makes you write the step first.

English Literature

Points to specific lines and stanzas. Asks what evidence in the text supports a reading and which devices the author uses. Always anchors interpretation in the page.

Persuasive Writing

Toulmin-style probing: what is the claim, where is the warrant, is the evidence sufficient, who is the audience, what is the strongest counter-argument. Strengthens — never rewrites.

What's inside

Engineered, not assembled.

Local-first by design

Everything runs on your machine. Embeddings, the vector index, the graph — local. Only the language model call leaves the box.

TurboVec compression

4-bit quantised vectors. A million-chunk corpus fits in roughly 4 GB of RAM, with faster search than uncompressed indexes.

Graph reasoning

Every chunk contributes entities and relations. Global queries map-reduce across community summaries — not against a single prompt window.

Citation discipline

In non-Socratic mode every sentence is checked against retrieved context. Ungrounded claims are detected before they reach you.

Rolling memory

Persistent facts and recent queries inject themselves into prompts when relevant — without bloating context for unrelated questions.

Honest by default

If your corpus doesn't contain the answer, Vaaani says so. It will not invent material to look helpful.
