Frequently asked

How does a pilot work?

One cohort, time-boxed. The children work through discovery missions; the engine builds a belief model of each and logs every decision with its reason. At the end we sit down and read the evidence together — belief maps, the causes behind each child's misses, and the engine's own calibration.

Is our students' data safe?

Yes. The teaching engine runs on CPU and the child's state stays in your tenant — it is never used to train a third-party model. We follow India's DPDP norms, with parental-consent flows for minors.

What do we actually get — a score?

No. You get a glass box: each child's belief state, the diagnosed cause of each error (including home-language transfer, named), and the engine's predicted-vs-actual calibration. Everything a decision is based on is inspectable — nothing is a black box.

Why is paid pricing "custom"?

Because we'd rather you see it work on your students before you commit a rupee. After the pilot we price per student (or per centre for chains), sized to your cohort. Multi-centre partners can discuss revenue share.
