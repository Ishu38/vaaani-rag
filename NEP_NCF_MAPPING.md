## 1. Stage alignment — Vaaani's bands sit on NCF's stages

NCF-SE 2023 restructures schooling as 5+3+3+4: **Foundational** (ages 3–8,
through Grade 2), **Preparatory** (Grades 3–5), **Middle** (Grades 6–8),
**Secondary** (9–12).

| Vaaani band | Grades | NCF stage | Vaaani's theme |
|---|---|---|---|
| Band 1 — *Language as play* | 1–2 | Foundational (tail) | oral, story-led, zero notation |
| Band 2 — *Language as pattern* | 3–4 | Preparatory | pattern-finding by ear and eye |
| Band 3 — *Language as system* | 5–6 | Preparatory→Middle | notation unlocks (default gate: G5) |
| Band 4 — *Language as science* | 7–8 | Middle | analysis, typology, language families |

**Talking point:** the notation gate's default (Grade 5) lands exactly on
the NCF Preparatory→Middle boundary — abstraction tools arrive when NCF
itself says children shift from concrete to abstract engagement. A school
moving the gate to 4 or 6 stays inside the Preparatory stage's span.

## 2. NEP 2020 — clause-level mapping

| NEP 2020 anchor | What it says (paraphrase) | What Vaaani does about it |
|---|---|---|
| **§2 (esp. 2.2) — Foundational Literacy & Numeracy** (→ NIPUN Bharat mission) | FLN is the "urgent national mission"; phonemic awareness and oral language are core FLN components | Band 1–2 is FLN-native: syllable clapping, rhyme families, sound-counting by ear, plural games — with the engine *enforcing* no premature abstraction |
| **4.11 — medium of instruction / home language** | Home language/mother tongue as medium until at least Grade 5, preferably Grade 8 | The Root Bridge Method's core principle: every word unit carries a Hindi/Bengali connection; tutor explanations are written to be followable by non-English-fluent parents; L1 patterns are taught *contrastively*, never suppressed |
| **4.12 — multilingualism's cognitive benefits** | Research shows cognitive advantages of multilingualism; young children pick up languages fastest | Vaaani's curriculum data honestly separates cognates (shared Indo-European ancestry, e.g. tri ↔ त्रि) from translations — children get real cross-linguistic insight, not decoration |
| **4.13 — three-language formula** | Flexibility, no language imposed; emphasis on India's language wealth | Vaaani is language-additive by design: connection data extends to any Indic language without re-engineering |
| **4.16 — "The Languages of India" project (Grades 6–8)** | A fun project/activity on India's languages, under Ek Bharat Shrestha Bharat | Band 4 IS this project, systematised: Grimm's law walked from pitā/pater/father; Indo-Aryan and Dravidian families mapped; the child's own languages placed on the tree |
| **4.6 — experiential, story-based pedagogy** | Experiential learning incl. storytelling-based pedagogy as standard practice | The Root Bridge unit flow is Character → Story → Picture → Pattern → Family → Discovery; jargon forbidden through Grade 4 |
| **4.34–4.41 — assessment reform (incl. PARAKH)** | Shift from summative/rote to formative, competency-based "assessment *for* learning"; track learning outcomes, not marks | The Cognitive X-Ray diagnoses the thinking pattern behind each error (12-type linguistics taxonomy); mastery is tracked per concept; spaced review resurfaces concepts before they fade; simulation trains calibration, not cramming |
| **§23–24 — technology in education (NETF, digital delivery)** | Technology for teacher support, personalisation, and reach — not replacement | Vaaani runs on its own engine (no child data to third-party AI providers), gives teachers diagnostic dashboards, and in school mode *refuses to replace the teacher* (enforced Socratic guardrails) |

## 3. NCF-SE 2023 — Language Education chapter mapping

| NCF-SE 2023 anchor | What it says (paraphrase) | What Vaaani does about it |
|---|---|---|
| **R1/R2/R3 framework** | Languages sequenced from the child's most familiar language (R1) outward | Vaaani treats R1 (Hindi/Bengali/home language) as the bridge into R2 (English) — the exact direction NCF prescribes |
| **"Multilingualism as a resource"** | The classroom's language diversity is an asset for learning, not an obstacle | Principle 3 of the Root Bridge Method, enforced in the error-diagnosis layer: mother-tongue transfer is a named, kindly-handled diagnosis with contrastive remediation |
| **Oracy before literacy; literacy through the familiar language** | Foundational stage prioritises oral language; reading rides on sound awareness | Band 1's zero-notation, audio-first design; the engine's notation firewall; audio narration/podcast modes for listening-led learning |
| **Conceptual understanding over rote** | Competency-based curricular goals; understanding *how* language works | Vaaani's whole premise: linguistics (how language works) rather than prescriptive grammar drilling; Feynman explain-it-back mode tests understanding by having the child teach the concept |
| **Continuous, low-stakes formative assessment** | Assessment woven into learning, informing the teacher | Every practice answer feeds the child's cognitive fingerprint; teachers see per-student diagnostic dashboards; no high-stakes marks anywhere in the product |

## 4. The three sentences to say in any NEP conversation

1. *"NEP asks schools to treat the mother tongue as the medium of learning
   until Grade 5 — Vaaani is the only tutor whose curriculum data literally
   encodes the child's home language as the bridge into English."*
2. *"NEP 4.16 asks Grades 6–8 to do a project on the languages of India —
   our Band 4 is that project, systematised and individualised."*
3. *"NEP's assessment reform asks for assessment-for-learning — Vaaani
   diagnoses which of twelve kinds of wrong an answer was, because the fix
   is different for each."*

## 5. Claims discipline

- Say **"designed in alignment with NEP 2020 / NCF-SE 2023"** — never
  "approved", "certified", "compliant with", or "endorsed by" (no such
  certification exists for this).
- The NIPUN Bharat FLN mapping applies to Bands 1–2 content; do not claim
  Vaaani is an FLN mission tool unless a school frames it that way.
- Verify paragraph numbers against official PDFs before print (see warning
  at top).
